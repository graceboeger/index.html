The World's Best Baked Ziti

Here is your guide to the gooey pasta dish of your dreams. I got this recipe from Nicole Burgess, a former chef at one of San Diego's oldest italian restaurants. She says this is a staple she's carried with her from her cooking career. "It's always a hit," said Burgess, "for children and adults alike, this is my one dish that never fails, and it's relatively quick and simple." Before you can start making this most perfectly cheesy meal, you'll need to make sure you have a few things in your kitchen.

  • One 16 oz package of ziti, rigatoni, or penne pasta
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 28 oz.can crushed red tomatoes (with juice)
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 packed fresh basil
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper, to taste

Here is a video tutorial if you'd like to follow along with me:

Before you get to chopping, you'll preheat your oven to 375 degrees farenheit. You'll also want to throw your pasta in salted water, and cook according to directions. Once your pasta is finished cooking, rinse it with cold water and set aside.

Next, you'll get to making the cheese mixture. In a large bowl, combine the ricotta, 1/2 cup mozarella, 1/4 cup parmesan, 1 egg, 1/2 cup chopped basil, 1 teaspoon salt, and one teaspoon ground pepper.

Once you have created your cheese mixture, you'll take your cooked and strained pasta and dump it into the bowl of cheese, then toss until it is all evenly coated. Then, heat two tablespoons of olive oil in a skillet to prepare for the sauce mixture. Add 3 cloves of minced garlic and 28 oz cn of crushed tomatoes to the skillet, and stir until all moisture cooks down.

Once your tomato mixture is ready, you'll pour half of it into your bowl of pasta, and toss until evenly coated. Then, pour the pasta evenly into a lighlty greased baking dish. Pour the rest of your tomato mixture over the top of your pasta, then top it with the remaining mozarella parmesan, and chopped basil.

Bake uncovered for 20-30 minutes until browned at the edges. Once removed from oven, let your ziti stand for 5 minutes to cool, then it's ready to serve!
